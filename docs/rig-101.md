# Rig 101 — what's on the bench

Each bench is a **fly-on-ball LED-arena rig**. A tethered fly walks on an
air-supported ball at the center of a cylinder of LED panels; the panels show
visual patterns, a camera watches the ball to measure how the fly walks, and an
LED delivers optogenetic light. Here's each piece.

## The LED arena (the panels)

- **G6 panels**, arranged **2 rows × 10 columns** wrapping a **full 360°**
  around the fly (`arena: G6_2x10`).
- Each panel is 20 × 20 pixels, so the ring is **200 pixels around** (azimuth)
  and 40 pixels tall — this is the "screen" your visual patterns play on.
- Patterns are `.pat` files stored on the controller's SD card. Arena Studio's
  Console lists them and can play/test any one.

## The controller

- A microcontroller box drives the panels and talks to your computer over
  **USB / serial**. In Arena Studio you reach it with **Connect** (Chrome/Edge).
- It also runs the **optogenetic LED** and the digital/analog I/O below.

## The optogenetic LED (light stimulus)

- An LED (ring/backlight) delivers the light that activates CsChrimson flies.
- It's driven from the controller's **Analog Out (0–5 V) BNC** through a
  **BuckPuck** current driver. The driver is *inverted*: lower voltage = brighter.
- The rig **idles the Analog Out at 5 V (LED off)**. Protocols set brightness as
  a **percent** (`ledDrive`), which Arena Studio converts to the right voltage.
- The LED has a turn-on threshold near **~4.2 V**; the intensity scale was
  rescaled so the old "5%" is now "1%" (just-on). A per-rig calibration table
  lives in the repo.
  > ✍️ **Instructor to confirm:** final LED %→brightness numbers per rig.

## FicTrac (the ball tracker)

- A camera images the air-supported ball; **FicTrac** software turns the ball's
  rotation into the fly's **turning, forward, and sideways** motion.
- A small **bridge** program relays that motion to Arena Studio for the live
  oscilloscope and for **closed-loop** experiments (the fly steers the display).
- Ball diameter is **~9 mm** (`ball_diameter_mm: 9`), used to convert rotation
  into mm/s. Full setup: **[FicTrac basics & config](fictrac.md)**.

## Air / ball support

- Airflow floats the ball so it spins freely. Flow is set with a regulator plus
  a **3D-printed roller clamp** (the most reliable option the lab found).
- Too much flow makes the ball jittery; too little and it won't float. Aim for a
  gentle, stable float.
  > ✍️ **Instructor to confirm:** target flow/pressure and how to set it.

## Digital I/O BNCs

- Two **"Digital IO (5V)"** BNCs on the controller. On course rigs, IO 1 carries
  a frame-scan debug pulse (handy on a scope) and IO 2 is off by default. You
  won't normally touch these.

## Two rig flavors

- **`cshl_g6_2x10_ball`** — the fly-on-ball rig (has FicTrac). Most experiments.
- **`cshl_g6_2x10`** — the same arena *without* FicTrac (used for
  hardware checkout like [p100](protocols/README.md)).

Arena Studio uses the rig's config to know what's present — e.g. it only offers
FicTrac closed-loop on a rig that actually has a ball tracker.
