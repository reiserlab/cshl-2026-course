# Tethering basics

To record a fly on the ball, you first glue it to a small pin ("tether") so its
head and body are held still while its legs walk freely. Good tethering is the
single biggest factor in getting clean data — a crooked or over-glued fly walks
poorly.

> ⚠️ **This page is a scaffold.** The specific glue, pin, holder, and anesthesia
> method for *this course* need to be filled in by an instructor — every lab does
> this a little differently. Steps marked **✍️** are placeholders.

## What you're aiming for

- The fly is glued **at the thorax** (top of the notum), head facing forward.
- The **head and thorax are rigid**; the **legs, wings, and abdomen are free**.
- The fly sits **level and centered** so it can walk naturally on the ball.
- **Minimal glue**, placed only on the thorax — no glue on legs, wings, joints,
  or eyes.

## Before you start (setup)

- Chill/immobilize the fly.
  > ✍️ **Instructor to confirm:** anesthesia method (cold plate / CO₂ / ice) and
  > timing.
- Have ready: the tether pins/holders, glue + curing light, a fine brush or
  pick, and a dissecting scope.
  > ✍️ **Instructor to confirm:** glue type (e.g. UV-cure), pin/holder type, and
  > light source.

## Step by step

1. **Immobilize the fly** and transfer it under the scope, dorsal side up.
2. **Position the pin** just above the thorax, angled so the fly will hang level
   when mounted on the ball.
3. **Apply a tiny drop of glue** to the tip of the pin (not the fly).
4. **Lower the pin onto the thorax** — touch the notum, keep the head free.
   Nudge the fly straight (head forward, body aligned) before it sets.
5. **Cure / let the glue set.**
   > ✍️ **Instructor to confirm:** cure time / method.
6. **Check the fly is free:** legs, wings, proboscis, and abdomen all move; only
   head + thorax are held.
7. **Let it recover** briefly before mounting.
   > ✍️ **Instructor to confirm:** recovery time.

## Mounting on the ball

1. Clamp the tether in the rig holder so the fly is **centered over the ball**.
2. Lower until the fly's legs just reach the ball and it starts to grip/walk.
3. Confirm it walks: you should see the ball move under it (and, once
   [FicTrac](fictrac.md) is running, motion on the oscilloscope).
   > ✍️ **Instructor to confirm:** target fly height above the ball.

## Good vs. bad tether — quick check

| Good | Bad |
| --- | --- |
| Glue only on the thorax, tiny amount | Glue on legs / wings / joints |
| Head free, body straight | Head glued, or fly cocked to one side |
| Fly walks steadily on the ball | Fly struggles, grooms constantly, or won't walk |

## Practice first

Tethering takes practice — use spare/practice vials before your experimental
flies. A fly that walks well on the ball is worth the extra attempt.
