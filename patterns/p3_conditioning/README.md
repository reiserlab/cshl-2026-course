# P3 Heisenberg/Dill conditioning patterns

Five G6 2x10, GS16, 200-frame full-azimuth movies for the planned P3
closed-loop conditioning experiment. Every movie contains cue A twice and cue
B twice, alternating every 90 degrees.

| ID | Pattern name | Cue A versus cue B | Historical source |
| ---: | --- | --- | --- |
| 1 | `p3_heisenberg_ts` | Upright versus inverted T | Wolf & Heisenberg 1991; Brembs & Heisenberg 2000 |
| 2 | `p3_heisenberg_high_low` | High versus low horizontal bar | Vertical-position feature experiments summarized by Tang et al. 2004 |
| 3 | `p3_heisenberg_slashes` | `/` versus `\` | Edge-orientation pair in Tang et al. 2004, Fig. 2C |
| 4 | `p3_heisenberg_relational` | Two oblique bars with their upper/lower orientations swapped | Relational pair in Tang et al. 2004, Fig. 2E |
| 5 | `p3_dill_random_checkers` | Two independently randomized quadrant-filling square textures | Dill, Wolf & Heisenberg 1995, Fig. 2 |

## Shared coordinate system

- Cue A centers in front at frames `25` and `125`.
- Cue B centers in front at frames `75` and `175`.
- Cue A reinforcement bands: `[[0, 49], [100, 149]]`.
- Cue B reinforcement bands: `[[50, 99], [150, 199]]`.
- One frame is 1.8 degrees; one quadrant is 50 frames / 90 degrees.
- The phase uses the course calibration that panel-column 3 is in front.

The first four pairs have equal dark-pixel area for cue A and B. The two random
textures each contain fifteen black and fifteen white 7x7-pixel squares per
quadrant, separated by one-pixel gaps. Thus their checks are 12.6 degrees with
1.8-degree gaps, the closest whole-pixel G6 approximation to the paper's
12.9-degree checks and 1-degree gaps. The first 180 degrees are repeated over
the second 180 degrees, matching Dill et al.

Generated by `protocols/make_p3_heisenberg_patterns.js` in the course-prep
workspace. The generator strictly reparses every `.pat` file and checks G6
geometry, 200-frame length, cue balance, and 180-degree symmetry.
